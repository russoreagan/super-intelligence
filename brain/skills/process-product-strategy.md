# Product Strategy (Definitive)

## Goal
Create a clear strategy that connects **market reality → product choices → measurable outcomes**.

## Core outputs (what “done” looks like)
- **Strategy summary**: who we serve, what we win on, and why now.\n+- **North Star + KPIs**: 1–3 core metrics + supporting metrics.\n+- **Prioritized roadmap**: themes + bets + sequencing.\n+- **OKR cascade**: company → product → teams.\n+- **Risks + assumptions**: what must be true; how we’ll validate.\n+
## Workflow
### 1) Market + competitive analysis (lightweight)
- Define target segments and jobs-to-be-done.\n+- Identify competitive alternatives (including “do nothing”).\n+- Map differentiation to customer value.\n+
### 2) Product vision and positioning inputs
- Write a crisp “why us” for the target segment.\n+- Ensure strategy is consistent with positioning/messaging.\n+
### 3) Metrics and KPI definition
- Pick a North Star metric tied to user value.\n+- Define guardrails (quality/reliability/cost).\n+
### 4) Roadmap and prioritization
- Convert strategy into themes and concrete initiatives.\n+- Prioritize by impact × confidence ÷ effort.\n+- Make tradeoffs explicit.\n+
### 5) OKR cascade
- Translate strategy into measurable OKRs.\n+- Ensure alignment scores/ownership are clear.\n+
## Templates
### Strategy one-pager
- Target customer:\n+- Problem:\n+- Differentiation:\n+- Key bets:\n+- Metrics:\n+- Risks/assumptions:\n+
### OKR cascade
- Company OKRs\n+- Product OKRs\n+- Team OKRs\n+
